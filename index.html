Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

What’s a Customer Account Takeover?

A customer account takeover happens when criminals steal customer information, such as usernames and passwords, to gain unauthorized access
to personal accounts, including online financial accounts. This can occur through phishing, social engineering, data breaches, or malicious software.

Preventing Account Takeovers

1. Watch What You Click

Avoid clicking on suspicious links or responding to suspicious emails. Always go directly to your financial firm's website or app to verify any information.

2. Use Strong Passwords

Create unique passwords for each of your accounts and change them regularly. Use a password manager to keep track of and protect your passwords.

3. Enable Multifactor Authentication

Enable multifactor authentication (MFA) for added security. MFA requires two or more types of verification, such as a password and a code sent by text
, or biometric identification like a fingerprint.

4. Maintain Device Security

Install and update security software on your devices. This includes antivirus, anti-spam, and spyware detection features. Regularly update your software
and devices to protect against new threats.

Safe Practices for Online Transactions

5. Use Your Own Device

Avoid accessing your financial accounts from public computers or devices. If you must use another computer, clear your browser’s cache and history
after logging out.

6. Browse Carefully

Ensure you are on a secure site when accessing financial accounts. Look for "https" in the URL and a closed padlock icon. Avoid multitasking on multiple
webpages while logged in to your accounts, and always log out when finished.

7. Be Cyber Safe When Using Wi-Fi

Avoid accessing your financial accounts over public Wi-Fi networks. Use trusted, encrypted networks and secure your home Wi-Fi with a strong password
and encryption.

Regular Monitoring and Prompt Action

8. Review All Correspondence From Your Financial Institutions

Regularly review your account activity and statements. Ensure your financial institution has your current contact information. Report any unauthorized
activity immediately.

Additional Actions

If your accounts are breached, change your login information immediately. Place a fraud alert on your credit file and seek additional resources on
identity theft from trusted organizations like the Federal Trade Commission’s IdentityTheft.gov.

By following these tips, you can significantly reduce the risk of cybercriminals accessing your personal financial information and protect your financial well-
being.
